What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a complex hormonal, genetic, and metabolic disorder that affects women of reproductive age. It’s one of the leading causes of infertility and is linked to long-term health risks including type 2 diabetes, anxiety and depression, cardiovascular disease, and endometrial cancer.

Why It Matters

PCOS isn’t just about irregular periods or acne — it's a lifelong health condition that can silently affect your fertility, mental well-being, and metabolic health if left untreated.

Fertility & Reproductive Health

PCOS is one of the most common causes of ovulatory infertility. Many women only discover they have it after facing challenges trying to conceive. [NIH]

Metabolic & Heart Risks

PCOS increases the risk of insulin resistance, type 2 diabetes, high cholesterol, and heart disease — especially if symptoms are ignored. [CDC]

Mental Health Impact

Women with PCOS are more likely to experience anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem — mental health support is essential. [NCBI]

Early Awareness Changes Everything

Timely lifestyle changes and monitoring can dramatically reduce long-term risks. But awareness is the first step — and many are still in the dark. [WHO]
